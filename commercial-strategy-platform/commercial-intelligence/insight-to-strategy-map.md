# Insight To Strategy Map

This map explains how Commercial Intelligence becomes Opportunity Model content.

## Flow

```text
Research
  |
  v
Commercial Intelligence
  |
  v
Opportunity Model
  |
  v
Engagement Type
  |
  v
Generators
  |
  v
Deliverables
```

## Mapping Rules

Commercial Intelligence should map insights into the Opportunity Model like this:

| Commercial intelligence output | Opportunity Model destination |
| --- | --- |
| Likely commercial priorities | `objectives`, `recommendations` |
| Implied executive KPIs | `objectives`, `measurement` |
| Customer segments | `audience`, `segmentation` |
| Market opportunities | `growthOpportunities`, `tam` |
| Revenue bottlenecks | `commercialChallenges`, `salesMotion`, `measurement` |
| Competitive pressures | `competitiveLandscape`, `risks` |
| Technology gaps | `technology`, `signals` |
| Sales and marketing alignment needs | `salesMotion`, `measurement`, `campaigns` |
| Risks | `risks`, `assumptions` |
| Assumptions | `assumptions` |
| Confidence levels | confidence fields attached to each inference |
| Strategic recommendations | `recommendations` |

## Section Mapping Guidance

### Opportunity

Use intelligence to clarify:

- the decision to support
- the primary audience
- the output targets

### Company

Use intelligence to summarize the organization in strategic language, not as a raw fact dump.

### Industry

Use intelligence to describe market pressure, category structure, and likely trends.

### Business Model

Use intelligence to explain how the company makes money and where commercial friction likely exists.

### Role

Use intelligence to frame the executive or functional mandate.

### Objectives

Use intelligence to translate observed pressure into a measurable business outcome.

### Commercial Challenges

Use intelligence to identify what is blocking progress.

### Growth Opportunities

Use intelligence to identify where the strategy should create lift.

### Audience, Segmentation, Personas

Use intelligence to identify who matters, how they differ, and what they need to believe.

### Journey, Signals, Campaigns

Use intelligence to move from market understanding into activation.

### Sales Motion, Measurement, Technology

Use intelligence to show how the strategy operates and how it will be inspected.

### Competitive Landscape, Risks, Assumptions, Recommendations

Use intelligence to preserve uncertainty, show tradeoffs, and define the recommended path.

## Directional Recommendation Rule

Recommendations should only become specific when the evidence supports the move.

If the evidence is enough to recommend a direction but not enough to lock a final plan, label the result as directional and preserve the assumption.

## Quality Gate

Before the Opportunity Model is written, confirm:

- each recommendation maps to a clear intelligence input
- each assumption is visible
- each confidence label is justified
- each strategic priority is grounded in evidence
- no internal priority is presented as fact
