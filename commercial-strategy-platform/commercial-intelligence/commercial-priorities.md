# Commercial Priorities

Commercial Intelligence infers the likely commercial priorities that should guide the Opportunity Model.

## Purpose

This file defines the priority categories that the platform can reasonably infer from research.

## Priority Categories

### Growth

Use when the business needs more:

- pipeline
- revenue
- account penetration
- market share
- conversion

### Efficiency

Use when the business needs:

- better productivity
- lower acquisition cost
- clearer handoffs
- faster cycles
- better focus

### Expansion

Use when the business needs:

- segment expansion
- geography expansion
- product expansion
- wallet share expansion
- customer expansion

### Retention

Use when the business needs:

- lower churn
- better renewals
- stronger adoption
- expansion readiness
- customer success improvement

### Differentiation

Use when the business needs:

- sharper positioning
- clearer proof
- stronger value narrative
- category distinction

### Measurement

Use when the business needs:

- better KPI visibility
- improved attribution
- cleaner reporting
- executive dashboard discipline

### Alignment

Use when the business needs:

- marketing and sales coordination
- operating rhythm
- shared definitions
- more reliable handoffs

### Transformation

Use when the business is changing:

- operating model
- customer strategy
- team structure
- systems
- market motion

## How Priorities Are Inferred

Priorities should be inferred from:

- job description language
- executive messaging
- business model pressure
- hiring patterns
- public performance signals
- sales motion complexity
- technology and data gaps
- competitor pressure

## Priority Output Rule

Each priority should include:

- the inferred priority
- the evidence behind it
- the confidence level
- the strategic implication

## Avoiding Overreach

Do not assume that the loudest public theme is the only priority.

When evidence suggests multiple priorities, preserve the tension instead of forcing a single story.
