# Evidence Standards

Commercial Intelligence uses evidence standards to decide whether a conclusion is credible enough to enter the Opportunity Model.

## Evidence Hierarchy

From strongest to weakest:

1. Direct company statement
2. Public filing, annual report, investor material, or official document
3. Job description or hiring material
4. Product, service, or customer page
5. Press, newsroom, or acquisition announcement
6. Credible third-party source
7. Strong market pattern or comparable-company signal
8. Single weak signal

## Minimum Support Expectations

### Fact

Facts require direct support from at least one strong source.

### Inference

Inferences require:

- at least one clear supporting signal
- an explanation of why the signal matters
- a confidence score

### Directional Recommendation

Directional recommendations require:

- a supporting fact set or inference set
- an explicit assumption when internal context is missing
- a visible confidence level

## Evidence Requirements By Topic

### Commercial Priorities

Use job description language, leadership messaging, or recent public changes.

### Executive KPIs

Use role outcomes, business model pressure, or explicit operating goals.

### Customer Segments

Use public customer examples, product positioning, vertical language, or market focus.

### Market Opportunities

Use industry trends, whitespace, segment fit, or expansion signals.

### Revenue Bottlenecks

Use sales motion complexity, funnel friction, acquisition patterns, or measurement gaps.

### Technology Gaps

Use hiring signals, stack clues, workflow pain points, or public tool references.

### Competitive Pressures

Use competitor positioning, pricing dynamics, market concentration, or differentiation gaps.

## Evidence Discipline

- Do not stack weak signals into a strong claim without saying why.
- Do not cite evidence that only loosely relates to the conclusion.
- Do not let a single anecdote carry a strategic conclusion without a confidence penalty.
- Do not ignore contradictory evidence.

## Source Notes

If evidence is incomplete, note:

- what is known
- what is inferred
- what is missing
- what would increase confidence

## Practical Standard

The standard is not courtroom proof. It is enough support to make a useful strategy recommendation without pretending certainty.
