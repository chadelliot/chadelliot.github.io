# Commercial Intelligence

## Purpose

This layer interprets the discovery findings into a clear commercial point of view.

The goal is not to restate the job posting. The goal is to turn the source signals into a practical strategy for improving Syneos Health's market research and analytics operating model.

## Commercial Thesis

Syneos likely needs a more scalable, insight-driven operating model that converts fragmented research inputs into executive-ready commercial decisions.

The real challenge is probably not "more data." It is the ability to:

- unify research inputs
- prioritize the right customer segments
- translate customer intelligence into decisions
- maintain repeatable KPI reporting
- support commercial and market access stakeholders with one disciplined workflow

## Strategic Interpretation

### Market Research Operating Model

Syneos should have a repeatable mechanism for:

- framing the business question
- choosing the right method
- combining qualitative and quantitative inputs
- validating evidence quality
- translating findings into action

### Customer Intelligence Framework

The company needs a clearer way to organize customer intelligence by:

- stakeholder type
- therapeutic area
- commercial intent
- decision stage
- evidence need

### Analytics Maturity

The public signals suggest moderate-to-advanced maturity, but the posting still points to workflow fragmentation.

That usually means the issue is one or more of the following:

- inconsistent source integration
- weak reporting cadence
- limited standardization of KPI definitions
- disconnected voice-of-customer inputs
- too much dependency on individual leaders

### Measurement Framework

The operating model should support executive reporting for:

- leading indicators
- market response
- customer movement
- segment performance
- account-level intelligence
- commercial activation status

### Voice Of Customer

VOC should not be a one-off research output. It should become a reusable operating input that informs:

- segmentation
- messaging
- commercial planning
- market access conversations
- executive dashboards

### Competitive Intelligence

Competitive intelligence should be connected to market movement, client objections, and segment prioritization rather than stored as a static report.

## Risks And Assumptions

### Risks

- The insight workflow may be too manual to scale.
- Stakeholders may not share one definition of success.
- Research may be strong but slow to convert into decision support.
- KPI ownership may be unclear across teams.

### Assumptions

- The role posting reflects an operating model gap.
- The right solution is a repeatable insight system, not a single dashboard.
- Leadership likely wants faster, cleaner commercial decision support.

## 90-Day Discovery Plan

1. Map the current research and analytics workflow.
2. Identify the highest-value stakeholder groups.
3. Audit the KPI set and reporting cadence.
4. Review existing VOC and competitive intelligence assets.
5. Define where insight creation slows down.
6. Establish the first priority segment or client context.
7. Draft an initial operating model and validation path.

## 12-Month Analytics Roadmap

1. Standardize research intake and prioritization.
2. Create repeatable customer intelligence segments.
3. Build a shared KPI and executive reporting framework.
4. Formalize VOC and competitive intelligence reuse.
5. Improve the decision workflow between research, analytics, and commercial leadership.
6. Expand the model across priority client or therapeutic contexts.

## Directional Recommendation

Treat the engagement as an operating model and decision-quality problem.

If Syneos can make research, analytics, and executive reporting more repeatable, it should improve both commercial decision speed and the quality of client-facing strategy.
