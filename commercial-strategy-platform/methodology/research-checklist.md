# Research Checklist

Use this checklist after the Discovery Engine and before generating Commercial Intelligence and an Opportunity Model for a platform output.

The checklist is designed to be fast and practical. It should confirm that the research is specific enough to support the approved strategy sequence without turning the process into exhaustive diligence.

## Intake

- [ ] Intake Engine output exists.
- [ ] Discovery Engine output exists.
- [ ] Company name is normalized.
- [ ] Engagement type is normalized.
- [ ] Job description URL, job description text, or notes are captured.
- [ ] Company website is normalized when available.
- [ ] Recruiter information is normalized when available.
- [ ] Hiring manager information is normalized when available.
- [ ] Missing information is explicit.
- [ ] Follow-up questions are explicit.
- [ ] Intake status is set appropriately.
- [ ] Discovery research priorities are explicit.
- [ ] Discovery source links are captured.
- [ ] Discovery confidence levels are captured.

- [ ] Desired output targets are confirmed.
- [ ] Target role title is confirmed when relevant.
- [ ] Job description has been captured or summarized when relevant.
- [ ] Proposal, consulting, dashboard, interview, cover-letter, or outreach objective is captured when relevant.
- [ ] Company website has been reviewed.
- [ ] Product, service, or solution pages have been reviewed.
- [ ] Investor relations, annual report, or public financial materials have been reviewed when available.
- [ ] Public customer, industry, or use-case pages have been reviewed when available.
- [ ] Recent press, newsroom, or public announcements have been reviewed when available.
- [ ] Recent acquisition announcements have been reviewed when available.
- [ ] Public evidence of marketing maturity or MarTech stack has been reviewed when available.
- [ ] Source URLs have been saved.
- [ ] Open assumptions are listed separately from sourced facts.
- [ ] Internal priorities are not stated as facts unless sourced or provided.
- [ ] Known or likely internal constraints are marked as assumptions to validate.

## Company Intelligence

- [ ] Company overview is concise and sourced.
- [ ] Industry is identified.
- [ ] Relevant competitors are identified.
- [ ] Customer segments are described.
- [ ] Public leadership priorities are captured when available.
- [ ] Recent acquisitions are captured or marked not found.
- [ ] Financial performance is summarized from public information when available.
- [ ] Existing marketing maturity is assessed.
- [ ] Probable MarTech stack is documented from public evidence or marked as inferred.
- [ ] AI opportunities are identified.
- [ ] Material risks are identified.
- [ ] Growth opportunities are identified.
- [ ] Sourced facts are separated from inferred recommendations.

## Commercial Intelligence

- [ ] Likely commercial priorities are identified.
- [ ] Implied executive KPIs are identified.
- [ ] Revenue bottlenecks are identified.
- [ ] Competitive pressures are identified.
- [ ] Technology gaps are identified.
- [ ] Sales and marketing alignment needs are identified.
- [ ] Each inference has a confidence level.
- [ ] Each recommendation is clearly directional when evidence is incomplete.
- [ ] Facts, inferences, assumptions, and recommendations are separated.
- [ ] The intelligence brief explains what would increase confidence.

## KPI Foundation

- [ ] Primary commercial objective is clear.
- [ ] Role-specific outcomes from the job description are captured.
- [ ] At least three business outcomes can be stated in measurable terms.
- [ ] The strategy distinguishes executive outcomes from team activities.
- [ ] Unknown assumptions are marked for validation.

## Audience Architecture

- [ ] Ideal customer profile is clear enough to describe.
- [ ] At least three meaningful customer segments are identified.
- [ ] Segment priority can be explained.
- [ ] Segment pain points are specific to the company, market, or role.
- [ ] Buying committee includes economic buyer, influencers, users, and blockers where applicable.
- [ ] At least three personas have distinct priorities and objections.

## Total Addressable Market

- [ ] Market definition is specific enough to avoid generic sizing.
- [ ] Priority market segments are identified.
- [ ] Geographic, vertical, account-size, or use-case boundaries are documented.
- [ ] Market-sizing assumptions are clear.
- [ ] Any numeric claims can be sourced or labeled as estimates.
- [ ] Map or regional data can be represented with simple locations or percentages if needed.

## Prospect Funnel And Revenue Prioritization

- [ ] Account prioritization criteria are defined.
- [ ] Revenue opportunity indicators are identified.
- [ ] Expansion, churn-risk, buying-readiness, or growth signals are captured where relevant.
- [ ] Intelligence can be converted into a recommended action.
- [ ] Insights are tied to campaign or sales prioritization.

## Prospect Funnel

- [ ] Major buying stages are identified.
- [ ] Buyer questions are listed for each stage.
- [ ] Content or proof needs are listed for each stage.
- [ ] Sales actions are listed for each stage.
- [ ] Likely friction points or decision delays are documented.

## Signal Intelligence

- [ ] At least four actionable signals are identified.
- [ ] Each signal is observable or operationally collectible.
- [ ] Each signal has a clear interpretation.
- [ ] Each signal has a recommended activation path.
- [ ] Signal ownership is clear where possible.

## Campaign Activation

- [ ] Campaign themes connect to the company's offer and buyer pressure.
- [ ] Target audience is clear for each campaign.
- [ ] Channels are practical for the target audience.
- [ ] Offers or calls to action are specific.
- [ ] Success metrics are measurable.

## Sales Motion

- [ ] Primary sales motion is identified.
- [ ] Sales play entry point is clear.
- [ ] Stakeholders are mapped to the sales play.
- [ ] Required sales assets are listed.
- [ ] Exit criteria are defined.
- [ ] The sales motion fits likely deal complexity.

## Return to Dashboard

- [ ] Dashboard summary connects strategy to business outcomes.
- [ ] Metrics include leading indicators and outcome indicators.
- [ ] Metrics can be inspected by leadership.
- [ ] Dashboard questions help identify whether the strategy is working.
- [ ] Metrics avoid vanity activity where possible.

## M+S Alignment

- [ ] North star metric is defined.
- [ ] Scorecard metrics have definitions.
- [ ] Owners are assigned where possible.
- [ ] Review cadence is defined.
- [ ] Marketing and sales handoffs are measurable.
- [ ] Measurement plan can support weekly or monthly operating reviews.

## System Evolution Assumptions

- [ ] First 90-day priorities are clear.
- [ ] 6-12 month scaling priorities are clear.
- [ ] 12-24 month maturity priorities are clear.
- [ ] Roadmap milestones are practical and sequenced.
- [ ] Dependencies, systems, or operating habits are identified.
- [ ] Evolution assumptions connect back to the KPI foundation.

## Model Readiness

- [ ] Content fits the canonical ten-section Commercial Strategy navigation.
- [ ] Each stage has enough content to render without empty cards or panels.
- [ ] Copy is concise enough for the existing Commercial Strategy design.
- [ ] Claims are sourced, estimated, or clearly framed as recommendations.
- [ ] Sourced facts, directional hypotheses, and recommendations are distinct.
- [ ] Copy does not pretend to know internal priorities.
- [ ] Strategy acknowledges that recommendations should evolve after internal constraints are learned.
- [ ] No section relies on a new page layout that has not been approved.
- [ ] Source URLs and open assumptions are ready to include in the Opportunity Model source metadata.
- [ ] Commercial Intelligence confidence scores are ready to carry into the Opportunity Model.

## Final Review

- [ ] The output would feel specific beyond organization-name replacement.
- [ ] The recommendations are specific enough for the target audience.
- [ ] The strategy is credible from research and the Opportunity Intake.
- [ ] Website/proposal content can be rendered by existing components or obvious visual mappings.
- [ ] Website outputs should preserve the existing visual design and interactions.
