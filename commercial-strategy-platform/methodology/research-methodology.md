# Research Methodology

This methodology supports creating Commercial Strategy Models from an Opportunity Intake and research.

The goal is not exhaustive diligence. The goal is enough structured insight to generate credible, specific, and useful outputs quickly.

## Inputs

Start with:

- Opportunity Intake
- target job description when relevant
- proposal, consulting, dashboard, interview, cover-letter, or outreach objective when relevant
- company website
- product or service pages
- leadership or about page
- investor relations, annual report, or public financial materials when available
- recent press or newsroom items
- acquisition announcements when available
- public customer or industry pages
- LinkedIn company context, when available
- review sites, technology pages, job postings, or public pixels that suggest marketing maturity or MarTech stack
- credible third-party market context, when available

Capture source URLs for traceability.

## Research Questions

Answer these before writing the Commercial Strategy Model.

### Opportunity Context

- What type of opportunity is this?
- Is the output for a VP Marketing application, RevHub agency work, Audaption consulting work, proposal, executive dashboard, interview preparation, cover letter, or LinkedIn/recruiter outreach?
- Who is the audience for the output?
- What decision or next step should the output support?
- What constraints are known from the Opportunity Intake?

### Company Context

- What does the company sell?
- Who buys it?
- What market or operating problem does it solve?
- Is the company B2B, B2C, marketplace, services, software, distribution, or hybrid?
- What geographies, verticals, or account types appear most important?

### Company Intelligence

- What is the concise company overview?
- What industry does the company compete in?
- Who are the most relevant competitors?
- Which customer segments does the company appear to serve?
- What public leadership priorities are visible from executive messaging, investor materials, press, or hiring?
- Has the company made recent acquisitions?
- What does public financial performance suggest about growth, margin, efficiency, or investment priorities?
- How mature does existing marketing appear to be?
- What MarTech stack is probable from public evidence?
- Where could AI improve growth, efficiency, personalization, research, operations, or measurement?
- What risks should the strategy account for?
- What growth opportunities are visible from public research?

### Role Context

- What outcomes does the job description emphasize?
- What functions will the role influence?
- Which metrics are likely important?
- Does the role focus more on growth, transformation, retention, market expansion, sales productivity, or operating discipline?

### Commercial Motion

- Is the likely motion enterprise, mid-market, SMB, partner-led, product-led, channel-led, or field-led?
- Is demand created by campaigns, relationships, procurement events, intent signals, renewals, or expansion?
- What sales cycle complexity should the strategy reflect?

### KPI Foundation

- What business outcomes should the strategy directly support?
- What measurable commercial objectives appear in the job description?
- Which objectives are executive-level priorities rather than team-level activities?
- What assumptions need validation before the strategy can be operationalized?

### Audience Architecture

- Which customer groups deserve separate treatment?
- Which segments are likely highest-value?
- Which segments are easiest to reach?
- Which segments need education before conversion?

### Buying Committee

- Who is the economic buyer?
- Who influences the decision?
- Who blocks the decision?
- Who uses the product or service day to day?
- What proof does each persona need?

### Total Addressable Market

- What markets, verticals, regions, account types, or use cases define the realistic opportunity?
- What public evidence supports market sizing assumptions?
- Which parts of the market should be prioritized first?
- Which assumptions should be labeled as estimates?

### Prospect Funnel And Revenue Prioritization

- What public signals indicate revenue opportunity, expansion, churn risk, or buying readiness?
- What account-level intelligence would help prioritize outreach?
- What patterns suggest a company is changing budget, leadership, footprint, or operating model?
- Which insights should affect campaign or sales prioritization?

### Prospect Funnel

- What buying stages should the strategy influence?
- What questions does the buyer need answered at each stage?
- What content, proof, or sales action helps move the buyer forward?
- Where are the likely points of friction or delay?

### Signal Intelligence

- What events suggest an account is ready for outreach?
- Which signals are observable from public data?
- Which signals should trigger marketing, SDR, or sales action?

### Campaign Activation

- Which campaign themes connect the company's offer to the buyer's current pressure?
- Which channels are practical for the target audience?
- Which offers or proof points should create meeting demand?

### Sales Motion

- What sales play fits the deal complexity?
- What entry points and stakeholders should sales prioritize?
- What assets, handoffs, or exit criteria make the play executable?

### M+S Alignment And Return to Dashboard

- What meetings, handoffs, and shared metrics would make the strategy executable?
- Where should marketing and sales coordinate?
- What should leadership inspect weekly or monthly?
- Which metrics belong in an executive dashboard?
- Which metrics should be leading indicators versus outcome indicators?

### System Evolution Assumptions

- What should be proven in the first 90 days?
- What should scale over the first 6-12 months?
- What should mature across 12-24 months?
- What dependencies, systems, or operating habits must be built over time?

## Evidence Standards

Use specific evidence when available:

- job description language
- named products or services
- customer segments described by the company
- public expansion announcements
- hiring patterns
- investor or annual report language
- case studies
- geography or location footprint
- partnership announcements

When estimating, make the assumption clear in the content or research notes.

Public research should support directional strategy. It should not pretend to reveal internal priorities, current pipeline reality, budget, team capacity, system constraints, customer data, or executive tradeoffs unless those details are provided or publicly sourced.

Every strategy should acknowledge that recommendations will evolve after internal constraints are understood.

## Personalization Rules

Strong outputs should include:

- the company name in meaningful strategic places
- the opportunity type and intended output audience
- industry context and competitive pressure
- role-specific outcomes from the job description
- segment names that match the company's market
- personas that match the actual buying committee
- public leadership priorities where available
- recent acquisitions, financial performance, marketing maturity, MarTech, AI opportunities, risks, and growth opportunities when they materially affect the strategy
- market assumptions that can be explained
- revenue prioritization connected to the prospect funnel
- buyer-stage logic that supports the prospect funnel
- campaign ideas connected to the company's offer
- sales plays that match likely deal complexity
- metrics that reflect the role's expected operating model
- explicit separation between sourced facts, directional hypotheses, and recommendations
- acknowledgment that the strategy should evolve after learning internal constraints

Avoid shallow personalization such as only changing the company name.

## Handling Unknowns

Do not invent facts that should be sourced.

Use cautious language for inferred strategy:

- "likely"
- "appears"
- "suggests"
- "recommended"
- "assumption to validate"

Do not use cautious language for generic operating recommendations that do not depend on unknown facts.

## Quality Checklist

Before generating the Commercial Strategy Model, confirm:

- the Opportunity Intake is clear
- the output targets are clear
- the company overview is concise and sourced
- the industry and relevant competitors are identified
- public leadership priorities are captured when available
- recent acquisitions and financial performance are reviewed when available
- existing marketing maturity and probable MarTech stack are assessed
- AI opportunities, risks, and growth opportunities are captured
- the role objective is clear
- the target customer is clear enough to segment
- at least three meaningful segments can be described
- the buying committee has at least three personas
- the total addressable market can be framed with clear assumptions
- revenue prioritization can inform the prospect funnel
- buyer-stage logic can be expressed with clear buyer questions and actions
- at least four actionable signals are available
- the campaign ideas are specific to the company and role
- the sales motion fits the likely deal type
- the executive dashboard includes measurable outcomes
- the measurement plan includes owners and cadence
- the 12-24 month roadmap has practical horizons
- source URLs are captured
