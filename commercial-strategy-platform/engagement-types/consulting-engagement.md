# Consulting Engagement

Consulting Engagement is the engagement type for advisory or implementation work where the output supports a client decision, diagnostic, roadmap, or operating model.

The Opportunity Model stays consistent. The Consulting Engagement changes the audience framing, required inputs, and deliverables.

## Audience Framing

Primary audience:

- client executive sponsor
- functional leaders
- implementation stakeholders
- buying committee members

The output should show a practical path from business objective to operating model, measurement, and execution. It should be clear where public research ends and discovery assumptions begin.

## Required Inputs

Required Opportunity Intake inputs:

- client or target organization
- consulting objective
- business problem or opportunity
- current-state assumptions
- known constraints
- stakeholder audience
- desired decision or next step
- available source materials
- desired deliverables

Helpful optional inputs:

- discovery notes
- stakeholder interviews
- existing dashboards
- current MarTech or RevTech stack
- sales and marketing process notes
- budget or timing constraints
- implementation capacity

## Deliverables

Common deliverables:

- proposal
- diagnostic brief
- executive dashboard
- operating model
- 12-24 month roadmap
- workshop narrative
- implementation plan

## Output Guidance

Emphasize:

- decision clarity
- practical sequencing
- operating constraints
- implementation path
- measurement cadence
- stakeholder alignment

Avoid:

- generic transformation language
- unsupported certainty about internal systems
- overbuilding the scope before discovery
- presenting roadmap assumptions as facts
