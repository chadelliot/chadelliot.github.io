# Executive Hire

Executive Hire is the engagement type for leadership opportunities, especially VP Marketing or senior commercial roles.

The Opportunity Model stays consistent. The Executive Hire engagement changes the audience framing, required inputs, and deliverables.

## Audience Framing

Primary audience:

- hiring leader
- executive team
- recruiter
- board-adjacent stakeholders

The output should demonstrate strategic judgment, commercial operating fluency, and role-specific relevance. It should not pretend to know internal priorities, current pipeline reality, budget, team capacity, or internal constraints unless provided.

## Required Inputs

Required Opportunity Intake inputs:

- target company
- role title
- job description or role brief
- known hiring context
- public company research
- public leadership priorities when available
- industry and competitor context
- current marketing maturity signals
- likely revenue motion
- desired deliverables

Helpful optional inputs:

- recruiter notes
- interview stage
- known executive stakeholders
- prior conversation notes
- resume positioning
- preferred outreach angle

## Deliverables

Common deliverables:

- Commercial Strategy page
- interview preparation brief
- cover letter
- LinkedIn/recruiter outreach
- executive dashboard narrative
- first-90-days discussion guide

## Output Guidance

Emphasize:

- fit for the role
- business outcomes
- commercial operating model
- executive-level thinking
- practical next steps
- questions to validate internal constraints

Avoid:

- sounding like a generic job application
- overstating public research
- claiming knowledge of internal priorities
- making the strategy look fixed before internal discovery
