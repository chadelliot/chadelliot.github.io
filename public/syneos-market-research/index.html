Chapter 02 — Segmentation

Know the market. Define the growth.

Segmentation is where strategy begins to take shape. Before we can define a growth objective, we need to understand who is in the market — which pharma and biotech teams already engage Syneos, who should, and what each group needs. The segments themselves answer the growth question: they tell us what to pursue, in what order, and with what motion.

"Segmentation turns a broad market into actionable groups. It tells us who we are speaking to, what they care about, how they buy, and where the largest opportunities exist — and in doing so, it defines the growth objective itself."
What segmentation reveals
Who the audience actually is
What each group needs and how they buy
Where the largest revenue opportunity sits
Which customers to grow, protect, or acquire
How to prioritize marketing and sales motion

Chapter 04 — Audience Architecture

Who gets the message. And what it says.

We structure the audience in two layers. First, we tier accounts by commercial value and stage to establish where effort should be focused. Then within each tier, we define the personas making the decisions — so messaging is personalized by business context and by the job each person is there to do.

"Tier tells us who matters most. Persona tells us what to say to each person inside that account."
